Fanatics / Topps

Rebuilding Growth for Marvel Collect

Diagnosing a major revenue decline, earning executive confidence, and rebuilding the operating system around how Marvel fans actually collected.

Role: Product Manager

Digital collectibles / Live product RF
01
Challenge

Revenue had fallen roughly 45% from its highs with no agreed-upon cause, and leadership needed an explanation and a credible recovery plan.

Leadership response

Diagnosed a content-capacity constraint, earned executive and board confidence, and built a cross-functional operating coalition around the fix.

Outcome

The decline reversed within six weeks; the app later exceeded its previous monthly revenue records by more than 30%.

01Context

Marvel Collect by Topps is a live digital collectibles platform serving a passionate, highly knowledgeable fan community. The product combines licensed Marvel content, collecting mechanics, a player-driven economy, limited-time releases, and a continuous cadence of events, offers, and live operations.

02The Problem

When I joined the team, the app's revenue had declined approximately 45% from its previous highs, with no clear consensus around the cause. The brand remained strong and the audience was still engaged, but performance had become inconsistent, and company leadership needed an explanation and a recovery plan.

The challenge was not only to identify the root cause. It was also to create alignment across departments, earn confidence in a high-pressure environment, and turn that diagnosis into coordinated action.

03My Role

As Product Manager, I was responsible for product development, feature ideation, the mobile economy, pricing, deals and sales, content launches, release strategy, and live operations. My scope also included working with Marvel and other licensing stakeholders, supporting trust-and-safety initiatives within the in-app economy, and coordinating the teams responsible for keeping the product engaging, reliable, and commercially sustainable.

04Key Actions
01

Diagnosed the real constraint

I began by analyzing customer behavior, purchasing patterns, release performance, production output, and fan response. The data showed that demand had not disappeared. The core issue was content and production capacity: the team could no longer consistently deliver the volume, variety, and cadence of releases the audience expected.

02

Built executive confidence

Diagnosing the problem was only the first step. Fixing it required buy-in from creative, production, licensing, finance, commercial leadership, and other stakeholders with different priorities and constraints. I presented the diagnosis and turnaround plan first to senior leadership within the Topps Digital division and then to the company's board of directors. I clearly explained what had changed, outlined the factors behind a decline that had begun before my arrival only weeks earlier, and presented a path to reverse it. In a high-stakes setting and under significant scrutiny, I remained calm, grounded the conversation in evidence, and took ownership of delivering the recovery.

03

Aligned the operating organization

I then built a cross-functional operating coalition around the plan. I established weekly check-ins with department heads and leadership, introduced clearer forecasting and performance metrics, and created a steadier operating cadence for production, approvals, inventory planning, and launches. These meetings were not simply status updates; they became a forum for resolving constraints, maintaining accountability, and keeping teams aligned around a shared outcome.

04

Strengthened the economy and live experience

Alongside the pipeline rebuild, I refined pricing, promotional offers, and sales strategy; managed live events and launches; partnered with licensors; and supported trust-and-safety work that protected the integrity of the collector economy. Fan behavior remained a central signal—not only what customers purchased, but how they collected, traded, responded to scarcity, and engaged with specific characters, artwork, and release formats.

05Outcome
6 weeksto reverse the revenue decline
30%+above previous monthly revenue records

The revenue decline reversed within six weeks. The app subsequently exceeded its previous monthly revenue records by more than 30%, supported by a healthier content pipeline, stronger cross-functional alignment, clearer operating systems, and a strategy grounded in how Marvel fans actually engaged with the product.
