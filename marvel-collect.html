The challenge

What needed to change

Marvel Collect remained a strong brand with an engaged fan base, but revenue had fallen roughly 45% from its previous highs and there was no agreed-upon explanation. Leadership needed both a credible diagnosis and a recovery plan.

My role

Where I owned the work

As Product Manager, I owned product development, mobile-economy decisions, pricing and promotions, release strategy, content launches, and live operations. I also partnered with Marvel and coordinated the teams responsible for keeping the app engaging and commercially sustainable.

Key actions

How I moved it forward

01

Diagnosed the real constraint

Customer behavior and purchase patterns showed that demand had not disappeared. The limiting factor was content and production capacity: the team could no longer deliver the volume, variety, and cadence the audience expected.

02

Built executive confidence

I presented the diagnosis and turnaround plan to senior digital leadership and the board, explaining what had changed and why the decline had begun before my arrival.

03

Rebuilt the operating cadence

I established cross-functional check-ins, clearer forecasts, and a steadier cadence for production, approvals, inventory planning, and launches.

04

Strengthened the live economy

I refined pricing, promotions, release strategy, and live events while using fan behavior as a continuous signal for product decisions.

Result

What the work produced

The revenue decline reversed within six weeks. The app later exceeded its previous monthly revenue records by more than 30%, supported by a healthier content pipeline, stronger alignment, and a strategy grounded in how Marvel fans actually used the product.
