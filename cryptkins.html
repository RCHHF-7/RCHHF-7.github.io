Cryptozoic — Original IP

Expanding an Original IP into a Scalable Brand

Championing Cryptozoic’s original Cryptkins IP during an ownership gap—building creative guardrails, expanding the universe, and developing commercially grounded product concepts.

Role: Product Manager

Original IP / Brand stewardshipRF
04
Cryptkinsoriginal IP stewardship
Challenge

When the collectibles leader left, stewardship of the company-owned Cryptkins IP became unclear—with no external licensor or brand bible to protect its identity.

Leadership response

Championed the IP, created creative guardrails, expanded the character universe and digital products, and pitched commercially grounded physical concepts.

Outcome

A stronger creative and commercial foundation that kept a promising IP on track during a period of organizational uncertainty.

01Context

Cryptkins was an original Cryptozoic collectibles property built around mythical creatures reimagined as stylized, chibi-inspired characters with a distinctive visual identity. The initial concept originated within the collectibles team and had already demonstrated potential as a company-owned brand.

When the leader of the collectibles department left the company, however, long-term stewardship of the IP became unclear. I believed Cryptkins had significant room to grow beyond its original format and took the initiative to champion its continued development.

02The Problem

Unlike Cryptozoic’s licensed products, Cryptkins did not come with an external licensor, established brand bible, or dedicated partner responsible for protecting its identity. Without clear ownership and documentation, the property risked losing creative consistency, internal momentum, and its potential to expand across new products and channels.

The opportunity was to treat Cryptkins not simply as an individual product line, but as a developing brand. That required establishing creative guardrails, organizing internal teams around a shared vision, identifying commercially viable extensions, and building a foundation that future teams could continue to use.

03My Role

I stepped into an informal brand-stewardship and product-development role while continuing to manage Cryptozoic’s digital-product work. I led in-house designers and producers, partnered closely with the lead Cryptkins artist, and advocated internally for continued investment in the IP. My efforts included creative direction, brand documentation, new-character development, digital and physical product ideation, financial analysis, and executive presentations.

04Key Actions
01

Stepped into the stewardship gap

When the department’s leader departed and ownership of the IP was unresolved, I recognized the property’s potential and stepped in—taking on an informal brand-stewardship role alongside my digital-product responsibilities and making the case internally for continued investment.

02

Created durable creative guardrails

Drawing on my experience working with major entertainment licensors, I helped lead the creation of a Cryptkins style guide that documented the property’s visual identity, character conventions, and creative boundaries. This gave artists and producers clearer direction while protecting consistency as the brand expanded.

03

Expanded the IP across digital formats

I also helped ideate characters for a second Cryptkins series and worked with the key artist to develop original stickers, digital trading cards, and 3D figures for Quidd. These products extended the IP into a new digital-collectibles audience while preserving the personality and visual language of the original line.

04

Developed commercially grounded physical concepts

Beyond digital products, I advocated for Cryptkins enamel pins and developed a collection system incorporating rarity tiers and convention-exclusive releases. I created a detailed P&L to evaluate production costs, inventory exposure, pricing, and revenue potential, then presented the concept and gathered feedback from company leadership.

05Outcome
Creative standardsdocumented for future development
Expanded universenew digital and physical concepts

The work gave Cryptkins a stronger foundation for future development: clearer creative standards, an expanded character universe, new digital content, and commercially grounded concepts for additional physical products. More broadly, it ensured that a promising company-owned IP continued to receive active stewardship during a period when its ownership and direction could easily have become fragmented.
