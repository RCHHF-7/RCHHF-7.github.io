Cryptozoic / Quidd

Building a Licensed Digital Product Operation

Building Cryptozoic’s licensed digital-product operation on Quidd from the ground up—licensor management, forecasting, production leadership, and a repeatable multi-brand launch model.

Role: Product Manager

Licensed digital / New channelRF
03
QUIDDlicensed digital products
Challenge

Quidd was a brand-new channel with no leadership, infrastructure, or processes—spanning multiple licensors with different rules, commercial terms, and fan expectations.

Leadership response

Built the operating model from scratch—folding compliance into workflows, creating financial and production visibility, and standing up a creative and production organization.

Outcome

A repeatable, multi-brand launch operation that strengthened licensor relationships and created a foundation for the company’s own original IP.

01Context

At Cryptozoic Entertainment, I initially worked in marketing across licensed games, collectibles, and entertainment products. As the company expanded onto Quidd, a mobile platform for digital collectibles, I stepped into a broader product leadership role—taking responsibility for developing, producing, and launching digital collections based on major entertainment properties.

The portfolio grew to include brands such as Rick and Morty, Steven Universe, Adventure Time, Naruto, Attack on Titan, and Outlander, each with its own audience expectations, contractual requirements, and brand standards.

02The Problem

Quidd represented an entirely new product channel for Cryptozoic. There was no established leadership structure, operating model, or set of processes for managing it. The channel needed someone who understood the company’s priorities and operating principles, could navigate the requirements of multiple licensed properties, and had the financial and analytical fluency to manage digital-product performance.

It also needed an internal advocate who could translate app metrics and complex financial data into a clear business case for executives, partners, and prospective licensors.

Launching licensed digital products required more than adapting physical collectibles for a new platform. Cryptozoic needed an operating system capable of supporting multiple brands, overlapping production schedules, detailed approval processes, and complex relationships among licensors, internal teams, and Quidd. Each property came with different creative restrictions, commercial terms, asset requirements, and fan expectations—and without clear systems, a missed approval, inaccurate forecast, or off-brand decision could delay a launch, weaken a partnership, or undermine customer trust.

03My Role

I led product development and production for Cryptozoic’s Quidd portfolio while serving as the primary point of contact for multiple licensors. I coordinated internal designers and producers, managed submissions and approvals, reviewed contracts and content guidelines, built financial forecasts and tracking tools, and worked with Quidd to ensure platform execution remained compliant with partner requirements.

I also owned key digital-product metrics, prepared executive presentations, and pitched prospective licensors—building the case for digital collectibles and positioning Cryptozoic as a knowledgeable, dependable partner.

04Key Actions
01

Translated brand rules into operating workflows

I learned the details of each licensor’s brand guidelines and translated them into clear direction for creative and production teams. Rather than treating compliance as a final review step, I incorporated it into product planning, artwork development, documentation, and launch workflows.

02

Built financial and production visibility

I built forecasting models and tracking systems that connected production capacity, release schedules, commercial terms, app performance, and expected financial results. These tools gave leadership greater visibility while helping designers, producers, licensors, and platform partners work from a shared plan.

03

Shaped products for distinct fan communities

Audience understanding remained central to the work. Fans of each property valued different characters, moments, artwork, and collecting behaviors. I helped shape collections that felt authentic to those communities while still supporting rarity, engagement, and commercial goals.

04

Scaled the creative and production organization

As the portfolio expanded, I managed a growing pool of internal designers and a team of producers, clarified responsibilities, resolved approval and delivery issues, and maintained trust among stakeholders with competing priorities.

05Outcome
Multiple brandssupported through a repeatable operation
New channelestablished from the ground up

Cryptozoic successfully established and expanded its licensed-product presence on Quidd through a repeatable operation capable of supporting multiple brands and simultaneous launches. The work strengthened licensor relationships, created clearer financial and production oversight, and gave the company a scalable foundation for continued digital-product development—including the expansion of Cryptozoic’s own original IP.
