The challenge

What needed to change

Quidd was an entirely new product channel for Cryptozoic. There was no established leadership structure, operating model, or shared process for managing multiple licensors, overlapping schedules, approvals, commercial terms, and fan expectations.

My role

Where I owned the work

I led product development and production for Cryptozoic’s Quidd portfolio, served as the primary point of contact for multiple licensors, managed designers and producers, built forecasts and tracking tools, and partnered with Quidd on compliant platform execution.

Key actions

How I moved it forward

01

Turned brand rules into workflows

I embedded each licensor’s requirements into product planning, artwork development, documentation, approvals, and launch workflows.

02

Created financial and production visibility

I built forecasting and tracking systems connecting production capacity, release schedules, commercial terms, performance, and expected results.

03

Shaped products for distinct fan communities

I used audience expectations to guide character selection, artwork, rarity, engagement mechanics, and commercial positioning.

04

Scaled the production organization

I managed an expanding pool of designers and producers, clarified responsibilities, and resolved approval and delivery issues across stakeholders.

Result

What the work produced

Cryptozoic established a repeatable operation capable of supporting multiple licensed brands and simultaneous launches. The work strengthened partner relationships, improved financial and production visibility, and created a foundation for continued digital-product development.
