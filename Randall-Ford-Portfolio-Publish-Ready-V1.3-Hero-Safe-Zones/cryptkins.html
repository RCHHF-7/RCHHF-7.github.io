The challenge

What needed to change

When the leader of the collectibles department left, long-term stewardship of Cryptkins became unclear. Without an external licensor or established brand bible, the property risked losing consistency, momentum, and its ability to expand.

My role

Where I owned the work

I stepped into an informal brand-stewardship and product-development role while continuing to lead digital products. I partnered with the lead artist, directed designers and producers, developed documentation and concepts, and advocated for the IP internally.

Key actions

How I moved it forward

01

Stepped into the stewardship gap

I recognized the brand’s potential and made the case for continued investment when ownership was unresolved.

02

Created durable creative guardrails

I helped lead the creation of a style guide documenting visual identity, character conventions, and creative boundaries.

03

Expanded the universe digitally

I helped develop new characters, stickers, digital cards, and 3D figures for Quidd while protecting the personality of the original line.

04

Built commercially grounded concepts

I developed enamel-pin and collectible concepts, created a detailed P&L, and presented the opportunity to company leadership.

Result

What the work produced

The work gave Cryptkins clearer creative standards, an expanded character universe, new digital content, and commercially grounded physical-product concepts—keeping a promising company-owned IP active during a period of organizational uncertainty.
