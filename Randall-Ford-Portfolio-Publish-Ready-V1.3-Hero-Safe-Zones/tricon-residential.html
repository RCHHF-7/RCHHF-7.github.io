The challenge

What needed to change

A major acquisition more than doubled Tricon’s single-family-home portfolio. Tens of thousands of residents needed to adopt unfamiliar tools, confirm required information, and establish payment methods while the resident app was simultaneously moving to a new payment integration.

My role

Where I owned the work

As Product Manager for the resident app, I owned the roadmap for the resident-facing onboarding experience and represented that workstream within the broader acquisition. The app was the primary interface for personal information, compliance steps, and first-payment setup.

Key actions

How I moved it forward

01

Designed from the resident perspective

I translated research, behavioral signals, and anticipated friction into product requirements, process flowcharts, and a structured onboarding journey.

02

Owned the roadmap and design iteration

I managed priorities and the backlog, directed iterative design work, and presented proposed experiences, tradeoffs, and risks to leadership.

03

Managed app dependencies

I gathered needs from payments, compliance, communications, support, and finance; mapped app-related dependencies; and surfaced risks that affected the resident experience.

04

Protected knowledge transfer

I worked with senior leaders and counterparts at the acquired company to understand existing resident processes and reduce product and transition risk.

Result

What the work produced

The resident-app onboarding effort reached approximately 95% adoption across 40,000 households within six weeks. Residents completed critical transition steps, first payments were supported reliably, and the app workstream contributed to a broader platform migration with more favorable commercial terms.
